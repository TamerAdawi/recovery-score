How It Works

The science and math behind your recovery score.

Heart Rate Variability (40%)

HRV measures variation between heartbeats. Higher = better recovery. Score compares today vs your 14-day average: ratio = today / baseline.

Resting Heart Rate (30%)

Lower RHR = better recovery. Uses inverse ratio: ratio = baseline / today. Below baseline = higher score.

Sleep Quality (30%)

7-9 hours optimal (base 60), 6-7h moderate (base 40), under 6h poor (base 20). Bonuses for baseline consistency and 7.5h+.

Why 14-Day Rolling Baseline?

Population averages are misleading. Your own 14-day rolling average adapts to YOUR physiology and detects relative changes that matter.

Apple Shortcuts

Shortcut passes 3 values via URL: ?hrv=45&rhr=58&sleep=7.2. The app auto-computes baselines from stored history. Sleep can be hours, minutes, or seconds — auto-detected.
