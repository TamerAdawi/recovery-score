How It Works

The science and math behind your recovery score.

Heart Rate Variability (40%)

HRV measures the variation in time between heartbeats. Higher HRV indicates better parasympathetic (rest-and-digest) nervous system activity. Your score compares today's HRV against your 14-day average: ratio = today / baseline. A ratio above 1.0 means you're above your personal norm — a strong recovery signal.

Resting Heart Rate (30%)

RHR is your heart rate at complete rest. Lower RHR typically indicates better cardiovascular fitness and recovery. The scoring uses an inverse ratio: ratio = baseline / today. When your RHR drops below your baseline, your score increases.

Sleep Quality (30%)

Sleep is scored on total hours with an optimal range of 7-9 hours. Hitting the optimal range provides a base score of 60, with bonuses for sleeping at or above your 14-day average (+20) and getting 7.5+ hours (+10). Below 6 hours triggers a low base score of 20.

Why 14-Day Rolling Baseline?

Population averages are misleading — a "good" HRV for one person is different from another. By comparing against your own 14-day rolling average, the model adapts to YOUR physiology. It detects relative changes that matter, not absolute numbers that don't.
